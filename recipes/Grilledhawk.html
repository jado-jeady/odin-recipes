Grilled Tomahawk Steak Recipe

Description:

The Grilled Tomahawk Steak is an impressive and indulgent dish that is perfect for special occasions or when you want to treat yourself to a truly flavorful experience. This recipe features a thick-cut, bone-in ribeye steak that is known for its exceptional tenderness and rich marbling. The steak gets its name from the long rib bone left intact, resembling a tomahawk axe handle.

Ingredients:

  • 1 Tomahawk steak (about 2 pounds)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Garlic powder
  • Any other desired spices or dry rub

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to high heat for a perfect sear.
  2. Season the tomahawk steak generously with salt, black pepper, garlic powder, and any desired spices or dry rub. Let it sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes.
  3. Place the seasoned tomahawk steak directly over the flame on the hot grill. Sear each side for a few minutes until a beautiful char forms, giving the steak that irresistible smoky flavor.
  4. Move the steak to a cooler part of the grill or reduce the heat to medium-low. Continue cooking until the internal temperature reaches your desired level of doneness. For a medium-rare steak, aim for an internal temperature of around 130°F (54°C).
  5. Once grilled to perfection, remove the tomahawk steak from the heat and let it rest for about 10 minutes. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in a juicy and tender steak.
  6. After resting, slice the tomahawk steak against the grain into thick, luscious pieces. Serve it with your favorite sides like roasted potatoes, grilled vegetables, or a fresh green salad.
